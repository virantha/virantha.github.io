FlashForge Creator Pro 3D Printer Review

I'm rather late to the 3D printing party, having just gotten my hands on a FlashForge 3D Creator Pro last month. After spending several weekends with it, I just thought I'd do a quick write-up on my research and experience as a complete newbie into the 3D printing field.

FlashForge Creator Pro

I actually think the FlashForge is a solid value for the money, basically being a clone of the MakerBot Replicator 2X, but selling for $1349 vs $2499. It's made in China, and can be shipped direct from there, but you can also get it next-day in the USA with excellent support from their Amazon storefront representative (Tang). I'll cover all the features in detail below, but in summary, this is a dual-extrusion printer, down to 0.1mm layer resolution, heated aluminum print bed (for ABS support), and metal enclosure.

1   Doing your research

I'll admit, I considered getting a 3D printer this time last year, but I hit some decision paralysis; it seemed like the 3D printer was evolving fast, and there were newer machines becoming available in "just a few weeks". Unfortunately, 3D printers are usually from smaller companies that may have trouble meeting their shipping deadlines, so you probably just want to go with what's available now, instead of the latest and greatest that will probably take longer than you expect. Case in point is the Solidoodle Press, a follow-on to the popular Solidoodle 4 entry level printer. [ref] I pre-ordered this at their special $399 price point back in August, when it was promised to ship mid-September. Sadly, it was later slipped to end of October, and then again to December (and, IMHO, most likely 1Q2014). [/ref]

By far, the best background material on 3D printing I found was this special issue:

Make: Ultimate Guide to 3D Printing 2014]

This will give you a solid introduction to the pros and cons of 3D printing, the different materials available, and reviews of most of the popular machines. I highly recommend you read this; you'll get much more information faster than you would with a bunch of web searches.
